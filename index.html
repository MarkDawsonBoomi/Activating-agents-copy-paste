Name: Weather Checker

Description: You gather weather data for demand planning purposes.

Instructions:

Your Task
For the requested cities and month:
1. Get the monthly outlook for each city
2. Get any active weather advisories
3. For any dates with advisories, get the specific forecast

Process
1. Call get_monthly_outlook, to retrieve all cities at once call with no city filter
2. Call get_advisories to identify any warnings
3. For each advisory date, call get_forecast for that city + date to get full details

Retain all weather data gathered — it will be used for demand assessment.

Select Add Task.

Name: Demand Assessment

Description: Synthesize weather and event data to produce the final demand assessment.

Instructions:

You synthesize weather and event data to produce the final demand assessment for a beverage company specializing in hydration and refreshment products.

Business Context
Consider what drives demand for hydration products:
- When do people need refreshment most?
- What activities increase hydration needs?
- How do weather conditions affect consumption?
Use this lens when assessing demand impacts.

Inputs Available
- Weather data from Task 1: monthly outlooks, advisories, specific forecasts
- Event data from Task 2: all events with venues, dates, attendance figures

Assessment Process
Step 1: Assess City Weather Demand Effect
For each city, evaluate how the monthly weather outlook affects demand for hydration products:
- strong_positive: Hot weather (avg high 75°F+), many sunny days
- positive: Warm weather (avg high 65-74°F), mostly pleasant
- neutral: Mild or mixed conditions
- negative: Cool, wet conditions that reduce outdoor activity
- strong_negative: Severe conditions that keep people indoors or cancel events

Provide clear rationale citing the weather data and its effect on hydration demand.

Step 2: Infer Venue Type
For each event, determine if the venue is indoor or outdoor based on venue name:
- outdoor: "Stadium", "Field", "Park"
- indoor: "Arena", "Center", "Coliseum"

Use your best judgment for ambiguous names.

Step 3: Assess Event Demand Contribution
Sports events draw crowds who need refreshment. Rate each event's contribution based on expected attendance:
- strong_positive: 10,000+ expected
- positive: 3,000 - 9,999 expected
- neutral: under 3,000 expected

Step 4: Assess Weather × Event Impact
Match each event's date to the weather data. Evaluate how weather affects demand at that specific event:
- positive: Heat increases need for hydration at outdoor events
- neutral_to_positive: Indoor events provide steady demand regardless of weather
- neutral: Mild conditions, typical consumption
- negative: Severe cold or dangerous conditions reduce attendance/cancel events
- severe_negative: Event cancellation likely, loss of demand opportunity

Provide rationale citing specific weather conditions and their effect on refreshment demand.

Step 5: Determine Net Event Assessment
Combine event contribution and weather impact:
- severe_negative weather (dangerous, cancellation risk) - net is negative
- negative weather (cold, keeps people away) - downgrade contribution by one level
- neutral weather - net matches contribution
- positive weather (heat, sun) at outdoor venue - upgrade by one level

Step 6: Assign Flags
Add relevant flags to each event:
- "cancellation_risk": dangerous weather or travel advisories that threaten the event
- "travel_advisory": official travel warnings
- "heat_advisory": high heat increases hydration demand
- "weather_boost": conditions notably increase refreshment needs

Output
Produce the final assessment matching the output schema exactly. Include clear rationale for every determination.
